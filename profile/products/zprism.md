## What Zprism is

Zprism is a macOS menu-bar productivity tool. Press **Control + `** to summon a keyboard-shaped panel where **every key binds to an app or a feature** — so you reach the things you use most by muscle memory, not by typing to search.

It's more than a launcher: screenshots are annotated in place and read with OCR, and a tray of utilities (clipboard, calendar, weather, color picker, window management) live one keystroke away. Native Swift, **fully local, no backend.**

## Not a search box — a keyboard

Raycast and Alfred give you a text field. Zprism gives you a **grid of keys**. Each physical key maps to a fixed position, so your hands learn where things are: `J` is always your editor, `M` is always Mail. Two independent layouts (left / right Option) double the grid. Click an empty key to bind an app or feature, drag to swap, right-click to rebind.

## How to use

1. **Press Control + `** to open / close the keyboard panel (the menu-bar item toggles it too).
2. **Click or press a key** to run its binding. Click an empty key to bind an app or feature, drag to swap, right-click to rebind.
3. **Default Control + A takes a screenshot** — drag to select → annotate in place → double-click or **Copy** to grab it (also saved to your Desktop); **OCR** reads the text.
